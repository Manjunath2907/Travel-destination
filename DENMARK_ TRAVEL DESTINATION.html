1. Jog falls - The second highest plunge waterfall in India

Jog Falls is created by the Sharavati river dropping 253 m (830 ft), making it the second highest plunge falls in India. If only the total drop is counted, it is the 10th highest waterfall in India after the Kunchikal Falls, Nohkalikai Falls with a drop of 335 m (1,099 ft) in Meghalaya

3. Sri Chamundeshwari Temple - Traditional Hindu temple located on the eastern edge of Mysore

The Chamundeshwari Temple is considered as a Shakta pitha and one among the 18 Shakta pithas. It is known as Krouncha Pitha as the region was known in Puranic times as Krouncha Puri. It is said that the hair of Sati fell here.
